# Input data for a crab-claw proa simulator — v0.1

## Files

1. **crab_claw_CL_CD_polhamus.csv** — lift (CL) and drag (CD) coefficient
   curves vs angle of attack alpha (0-90 deg), for two sail apex angles:
   45 and 60 deg.
2. **driving_force_vs_AWA.csv** — driving force coefficient (Cdf) vs
   apparent wind angle, crab claw vs Bermuda rig.
3. **example_proa_parameters.csv** — example boat parameterisation
   (order of magnitude per Gary Dierking's designs: T2 / Wa'apa).

## Methodology and data provenance

### CL/CD curves (file 1) — semi-empirical model
Lacking access to raw wind-tunnel tables, the curves were generated with
the **Polhamus suction analogy** (Polhamus, NASA TN D-3767, 1966) — the
standard vortex-lift model for low-aspect-ratio delta wings:

    CL = Kp*sin(a)*cos^2(a) + Kv*cos(a)*sin^2(a)
    CD = CD0 + CL*tan(a)      (full loss of leading-edge suction)

where:
- AR = 4*tan(apex angle / 2) — aspect ratio of the equivalent delta,
- Kp = 2*pi*AR / (2 + sqrt(AR^2 + 4)) — potential-flow component,
- Kv = pi — vortex component,
- CD0 = 0.06 — parasitic drag (spars, rigging; tunable parameter).

**Calibration / validation:** at a 45-deg apex angle the model gives
CL ~1.72 at alpha = 35 deg and CL_max ~1.88 at alpha ~42 deg, consistent
with Marchaj's wind-tunnel results (Sail Performance, 1996): driving
force coefficient ~1.7 at a 90-deg heading angle (vs ~0.9 for a Bermuda
mainsail).

**Known limitation:** the full-suction-loss model underestimates L/D at
small angles of attack (gives ~3-4, while practice indicates up to ~8
for round spars). Tuning options: reduce CD0 and/or introduce a partial
suction factor (0 < s < 1) in the induced-drag term.

### Cdf vs AWA (file 2)
The anchor points at AWA = 90 deg come from Marchaj's published results
(crab claw ~1.7; Bermuda ~0.9). Intermediate values are an **estimate**
of the curve shape based on descriptions in the literature — to be
replaced with source-table data once the full texts are obtained.

### Boat parameters (file 3)
Order of magnitude per: G. Dierking, "Building Outrigger Sailing Canoes"
(International Marine, 2008) — the T2 and Wa'apa designs. Values to be
refined from the plans of a specific boat.

## Sources for obtaining the full tables (next steps)

- C.A. Marchaj, "Sail Performance: Techniques to Maximise Sail Power"
  (Adlard Coles, 1996; ISBN 0-07-141310-3) — chapters on traditional
  rigs; force charts for the crab claw. Available in libraries.
- Di Piazza A., Pearthree E., Paillé F. (2014), "Wind tunnel measurements
  of the performance of canoe sails from Oceania", Journal of the
  Polynesian Society 123(1): 9-28, DOI: 10.15286/jps.123.1.9-28. Open
  deposit: HAL (hal-01069676) — browser access (automated download
  blocked). 10 rigs, lift/drag measurements — best independent data.
- Irwin G., Flay R.G.J., Dudley L., Johns D. (2023), Archaeology in
  Oceania 58: 74-90, DOI: 10.1002/arco.5277 — open access (Wiley);
  Cdf, Csf, Crm coefficients of oceanic sails + U/V hull tests
  (CFD: Boeck et al. 2012; towing tank: Flay et al. 2019).
- Flay et al., "Hydrodynamics of Three Slender Models Resembling Pacific
  Canoe Hulls" — side force generated by a boardless hull (the proa case).
- Polhamus E.C. (1966), NASA TN D-3767 — suction analogy for delta wings
  (theoretical basis of the model used); NACA data for sharp-leading-edge
  deltas: L/D up to ~13:1.
- Schacht M., "Proa Rig Options: Crab Claw" (proafile.com) — mechanics of
  spilling lines (brails): leeward brail flattens the sail and cuts lift;
  windward brail forces a deep curve, cutting drive and redirecting the
  remaining force upward, reducing the overturning moment; both = furl.
- Practitioners' reports (WoodenBoat, Sailing Anarchy, YBW forums):
  camber ~1:5 raises CL by ~35% on close courses; brails degrade windward
  ability while depowering; brailing doubles as a "stop" during shunts.

## Note on data character
Values marked "estimated" are synthetic (model + literature calibration),
not raw measurement data. They are suitable for a v0.1 prototype; before
final simulator validation they should be replaced with source data.
